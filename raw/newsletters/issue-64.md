## RC2014 Orton 3C

As teased last month, the [RC2014 Orton 3C](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-orton-3c/>) was recently launched. The first batch has sold out, with more kits available at the end of this week.   
This 3 chip computer is based on the late Karen Orton's Minimal Z80. With just a Z80, 32k RAM and 1 logic chip, this can, and indeed, _must_ , be programmed via toggle switches and LEDs. Even in this pure form, it can still handle serial at 9600 baud!  
   
|  |  [ ![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/4c3e62be-cdf2-c44d-39b2-963f47becdae.jpg) ](<https://www.tindie.com/products/shieladixon/tmsemu3-graphics-card-designed-for-rc2014/>)  
---  
Even with such optional extra modern luxuries like a [ROM](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-orton-3c-16k-rom/>) or [I/O Module](<https://z80kits.com/shop/universal-front-panel-switch-and-led-for-romwbw/>), this is not for the feint hearted. But for those that are willing to toggle in raw Z80 assembly commands it can be so rewarding.  
The optional [cherry veneer front panel](<https://z80kits.com/shop/orton-3c-laser-cut-cherry-ply-front-panel/>) fits the ubiquitous Blue Box enclosure for a stunningly beautiful finish.

## RC2014 Assembly

The inaugural RC2014 Assembly took place earlier this month at [The National Museum of Computing](<https://www.tnmoc.org/>), and what a fantastic day it was!  
Almost all the tickets sold out, which meant a wonderfully busy day with lots of you from the RC2014 community.  There were a few traders there, including [Mr Gelee](<https://www.tindie.com/stores/mrgelee/>) with his heat sensor display, [Extreme Kits](<https://extkits.co.uk/product/rc2040/>) with their tiny, and not so tiny, RC2040 emulated kits, and [Steve Cousins](<https://www.tindie.com/stores/tindiescx/>) with some of his lovely RCBus kits.  
Exhibitors included Peter Onion with his [PicoVDU](<https://groups.google.com/g/rc2014-z80/c/_3_g7C2zKKc/m/aE9Ddn6jBAAJ>) amongst other things. Shiela Dixon had [MIDI](<https://www.tindie.com/products/shieladixon/midi-module-designed-for-rc2014/>) sound related stuff. And Nathan had an awesome hand built Z80 machine that kept on threatening to being nearly close to working!  
An hourly lightning talk kept everybody entertained with such talks as Chris Swan and his [TMS9995](<https://blog.thestateofme.com/2019/06/01/retro-computing-final-touches-for-tms9995-on-rc2014/>) based RC2014. The highlight for many though was the live video linkup with Wayne Warthen talking about the history of RomWBW and how it came in to being.  
Oh, and Yours Truely was there too with some bits and bobs.  
A huge Thank You goes to TNMOC and the volunteers that ensured things all went without a hitch, and to Peter Onion for all the other work involved.

## RC2014 Assembly Badge

All the attendees at the RC2014 Assembly got a name badge so we knew who they were. And in the great Badgelife tradition, it did more than show their name. This 38 channel logic analyser can connect to the RC2014 Standard Bus and indicate the state on SMD LEDs around the perimeter. There's a full write-up of the badge on the [RC2014 website.](<https://rc2014.co.uk/modules/other-modules/rc2014-assembly-badge-v1-0/>)

## 16C550 Twin UART

Yet another dual serial module is now available on z80kits. The [16C550 Twin UART](<https://z80kits.com/shop/16c550-twin-serial-module/>) features either one or two 16C550 chips, giving you even more flexibility on your RC2014. With support for up to 8 UARTs on RomWBW, you'll never be short of somewhere to plug another device in to!
