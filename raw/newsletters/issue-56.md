## Blue Box Rear Panel

After several iterations and tweaks, the [Rear Panel](<https://z80kits.com/shop/blue-box-rear-panel-kit-110x250x190/>) for the 110x250x190 Blue Box enclosure is finally available. Well, nearly. Orders will be taken from Friday 13th. This design features a matching PCB to the [Front Panel ](<https://z80kits.com/shop/blue-box-front-panel-switch-and-led-for-romwbw/>)kit and has a combination of open access to the RC2014 as well as an array of [Port Covers](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rear-panel-port-cover/>) to bring out the connections you need.   
|  |  ![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/072e56d9-3819-8284-ad5b-8e1f851e9db8.jpg)  
---  
In addition to the choice of port covers, an Enhanced Bus connector will allow you to plug in an extra module outside of your case.

## Retro Challenge 2024/10

I took part in my first Retro Challenge 10 years ago now, building a little Z80 based computer that I used the [#RC2014](<https://oldbytes.space/tags/RC2014>) hashtag for. (See [my original entry](<https://sowen.com/653/retro-challenge-2014/>)!). Well, for the 10th anniversary, not only will there be a specific RC2014 section to enter, but I will be sponsoring a prize for the winner of that section. It will be RC2014 related and pretty special. Keep an eye on [#RC2024](<https://oldbytes.space/tags/RC2024>) to find out. Things kick off on 1st October, so you haven't got long to get your entry in. Sign up [here](<https://www.retrochallenge.org/2024/08/rc202410-registration-is-open.html>) and good luck!

## 3D Printed RC2014 Micro Case

This neat little 3D printed case was designed by [Robin Wilson](<https://mastodon.me.uk/@robintw/113092258941229490>) and is a great way to protect your RC2014 Micro or RC2014 Micro Plus. You can print your own with the files on [MakerWorld](<https://makerworld.com/en/models/616909#profileId-540495>)  
.

## USB C Coming to the RC2014

Despite our love for old fashion retro stuff here at RC2014 Towers, sometimes it is nice to keep up with the times. Word on the street is that all the cool kids use USB C these days.  
  
So if you buy a full RC2014 kit from [z80kits.com](<https://z80kits.com/product-category/basekits/>), the optional USB adapter supplied will no longer be the old Micro USB connector as we're switching to USB C.  
  
A big thanks to [8086.net](<https://8086.net/products>) for the update.

## 3D Printable Expansion Box

This great addition to the Blue Box enclosure was designed by SocketWrench to house the mains adapter and VGA to HDMI converter. Read about the [design process](<https://deninet.com/blog/2024/09/08/printable-expansion-box-rc2014>) and download the [stl files](<https://github.com/socketwench/rc2014-psu-case/blob/main/panel-rear.stl>) to print your own.  
And if you have done anything with your Blue Box enclosure or Front Panel kit that you would like to share, please [get in touch](<mailto:rc2014@sowen.com?subject=Newsletter%20Feedback>)
