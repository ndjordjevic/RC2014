# Pageable ROM Module

After a couple of [PEBKAC](http://www.computerhope.com/jargon/p/pebkac.htm) issues, the Pageable ROM Module should be available in the Tindie store later this week.  This offers significant improvements over the existing ROM Module, such as

  * Large selection of ROM/PROM/EPROM/EEPROM supported
  * 1,2,4,8,16 or 32K ROM pages supported to allow you to get the most out of your chip
  * Easy selection of which page to use from jumpers
  * ZIF socket option
  * Works in conjunction with the [64k RAM Module](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/64k-ram-module-for-rc2014-z80-homebrew-computer/) to allow the ROM to be paged out and the RAM to be paged in

# [ATMEGA Programmer for Bus Pirate](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/atmega328-programmer-for-bus-pirate-36/)

Ok, so this isn't technically a RC2014 product - However, it is what I designed to make my life easier programming the AVRs in the RC2014 [SD Memory Dump Module](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/rc2014-sd-memory-dump-module/) and [Universal Micro Keyboard](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/universal-micro-keyboard/).  It's a simple easy way to plug in raw ATMEGA328 chips in to your Bus Pirate 3.6 then burn the bootloader on and program them.  Available in the Tindie store now!
