## RC2014 At Hackaday Supercon!

Earlier this month was the [Hackaday Supercon](https://hackaday.com/2018/11/08/it-happened-at-supercon-six-days-of-fun-in-a-three-day-con/) in Pasadena, and this event certainly lived up to the Super part of its name!  The free [RC2014 Micro](https://www.tindie.com/products/semachthemonkey/rc2014-micro-single-board-z80-computer-kit) that some people got in their Tindie Goody Bag was certainly a big hit too, and it's great to welcome so many new people in to the RC2014 community!

## RC2014 front Panel

If programming with a keyboard is too easy for you, [James Stanley](https://incoherency.co.uk/blog/stories/rc2014-frontpanel.html) has designed an Altair-inspired front panel for his RC2014. This is an amazing build, and really harks back to the early computig days   
---  
|  |  [ ![](RC2014%20Newsletter%20-%20Issue%2025_files/ebc682ff3af87fc194e43758d3b8cc05.png) ](https://youtu.be/cv958j9iXyg)  
---  
Rob Dobson has updated his popular [Bus Raider](https://www.tindie.com/products/robdobson/busraider-2-graphics-wifi-debug-kit-for-rc2014/) module. Now you can get memory mapped graphics, ZX Spectrum or TRS80 support and more on your RC2014 without having to solder surface mount components.
