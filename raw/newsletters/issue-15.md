## RCade

If you're looking for a smart all-in-one cabinet to house your RC2014, then the [Picade](https://shop.pimoroni.com/products/picade) cabinet from Pimoroni is worth looking in to.  Whilst it's designed for a Raspberry Pi and comes with joystick and big coloured buttons, with just a few laser cut parts, it can be turned in to a neat home for your RC2014.  A full writeup is on the [RC2014 Blog](https://rc2014.co.uk/1727/rcade-an-arcade-cabinet-for-rc2014/), and the laser cut files can be downloaded from [Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3083440)

## Electromagnetic Field

[EMF Camp](https://www.emfcamp.org/about) was absolutely awesome this year!  It was great to meet lots of the people that got their RC2014 at EMF Camp 2 years ago, as well as many of you from the RC2014 community.  The RC2014 workshop on Friday night was a huge success, and also gave me the benefit of some troubleshooting on Satruday morning!  The RC2014 stall on the night market was very popular, probably helped by the Blade Runner-esque atmosphere in the [Nullsector](https://benjamin.computer/posts/2018-09-10-EMF-Cybar.html).  Bring on 2020!

## Retro Computer Festival

If you can get along to the [Centre For Computing History](http://www.computinghistory.org.uk/det/43210/Retro-Computer-Festival-2018-15th-16th-September/) in Cambridge this weekend, 15-16th September, you're in for a great time.  Not only will there be a RC2014 display, but so much other retro computing goodness!  Make sure you come and say Hi, and remind me of your name as it's getting harder and harder to keep track.
