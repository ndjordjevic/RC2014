## RC2014 Zed II and Zed Pro II

Finally the [RC2014 Zed II](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-zed-ii/>) and [RC2014 Zed Pro II](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-zed-pro-ii/>) are live on z80kits. These successors to the RC2014 Zed and RC2014 Zed Pro were designed with [RomWBW 3.6.0](<https://github.com/wwarthen/RomWBW/releases/tag/v3.6.0>) in mind and have a full range of storage and options for a simplified buying experience.    
![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/79c5a857-a86a-35b9-68d5-d810d1654d8c.jpg)  
---  
The RC2014 Zed II starts at a much lower price than its predecessor, with only a single UART and single speed clock, whilst the RC2014 Zed Pro II comes with twin UARTs and multispeed dual clock by default. Both kits can be supplied with CF, USB, IDE or SD storage, real time clock, Why Em-Ulator sound and VGA serial terminal

## Electromagnetic Field 2026

July 16th-19th doesn't seem that far away now, so people are making plans to head to Eastnor, UK for [EMF](<https://www.emfcamp.org/about>) again. This bi-annual geek fest is a highlight in the RC2014 calendar, and this year is no exception. RC2014 kits will be available in the Night Market in Null Sector, and RC2014 workshops will be run in the Hacking Village.  
  
The [Homebrew, Historical and Retro Computing Group](<https://www.meetup.com/london-retro-computing/>) will be running their own village at EMF and would love you to bring along your RC2014 kits too. More details [here](<https://www.emfcamp.org/villages/2026/67>).

## Tindie Ongoing Maintenance

At the time of writing, Tindie has been undergoing "scheduled maintenance" and been unavailable for 6 days with only the vaguest of communication on social media. Whilst this has little direct impact on RC2014 products since z80kits was set up a few years ago, it is hugely frustrating for sellers and potential customers of 3rd party compatable kits.  
Many sellers have turned to alternative outlets or marketplaces. Even if/when Tindie is back on line, I suggest reaching out to sellers and asking where they would prefer you buy their kits.

Read about it and watch the video on her blog [here](<https://newstuffforoldstuff.blogspot.com/2026/04/toggling-etch-sketch-program-into-orton.html>).
