# It's Show And Tell Time

The Maker Faire season got underway at the start of April in Edinburgh. A good time was had by all and I got to meet up with several RC2014 owners, some of which I remembered from last year.  
Newcastle was the host for the UK Maker Faire again this year. Not only was there a good turn out of RC2014 owners, but [Tynemouth Software](https://www.tindie.com/stores/tynemouthsw/) (pictured) and [Dtronixs](https://www.tindie.com/stores/DTronixs/) meant that there was a good selection of RC2014 hardware on show too! A big shout out goes to Team Tindie too for hosting a great after show party

## 512k ROM 512k RAM Module

Those of you that want to run CP/M with extra whistles and bells will be glad to hear that the new [512k ROM 512k RAM Module](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/512k-rom-512k-ram-romwbw-module-for-rc2014/) is now here and comes with [RomWBW ](https://github.com/wwarthen/RomWBW)preinstalled.  
This, not only gives you a more advanced CP/M operating system, but it supports more hardware, such as a floppy drive interface, real time clock, or Ed Brindleys [IDE Adapter](https://www.tindie.com/products/edbrindley/ide-adapter-pcb-for-the-rc2014-computer).

## Small Computer Monitor

If you have been following the development of [SCMonitor](https://smallcomputercentral.com/projects/small-computer-monitor/) on the [RC2014 Google Group](https://groups.google.com/forum/#!topic/rc2014-z80/vKCQSUEmaTc) then you'll know that it now has an official release. This is a great monitor for the RC2014 with many great features and I'm excited to announce that from 1st June it will be included on all RC2014 64k ROMs by default
