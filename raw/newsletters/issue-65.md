## RC2014 Orton 3C-PO

This month there is not just one, but two new products for the [Orton 3C](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-orton-3c/>). The first is the [3C-Plus Output](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-orton-3c-po-shim/>), a simple shim that will eliminate memory corruption on systems that have a lot of I/O activity going on.  Most users won't need this, but if you're a power user, it could really help.   
|  |  [ ![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/7cafd0fa-0317-ac95-625a-497d0cfcfee7.jpg) ](<https://www.tindie.com/products/shieladixon/tmsemu3-graphics-card-designed-for-rc2014/>)  
---  
Second is a [39SF010 RomWriter](<https://z80kits.com/shop/39sf010-programmer-rom-writer-for-orton-3c/>) that is designed specifically for the ROM in the Orton 3C. This is essentially the same as the 30SF040 RomWriter for RomWBW but with different firmware and a green PCB so that you don't mix them up.

## Retro Computing Festival

RCF returns to the Computing History Museum on [15th](<https://www.computinghistory.org.uk/det/74558/Retro-Computer-Festival-2025-\(Gaming-Edition\)-Saturday-15th-November/>) / [16th November](<https://www.computinghistory.org.uk/det/74909/Retro-Computer-Festival-2025-\(Gaming-Edition\)-Sunday-16th-November/>) and this year it is the Gaming Edition. That means there will be RC2014 machines set up with Snake, Tut-Tut, 3D Monster Maze, TMSnake and Mazogs. And maybe even Kill The Bit on an Orton 3C! Tickets normally sell out, so pre-booking is advised.

## PeterO's Orton 3CX

It is always interesting to see how people use their RC2014 kits, but Peters Orton 3CX (The X stands for eXpanded. Or eXtreme. Or eXcellent) is a masterpiece of eXcess! Take a look at it in action on [BlueSky](<https://bsky.app/profile/peteronion.bsky.social/post/3m3dqwwcij22j>). When Jeff Goldblum saw it he was heard to say "Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether they could, they didn’t stop to think if they should."

## Retro Challenge 2024 Results

They might be 363 days late, but the full list of results from [Retro Challenge 2024](<https://www.retrochallenge.org/2025/10/rc202410-resultsless-than-year-late.html>) have finally been announced with [Tim Holyoake](<https://z80.timholyoake.uk/retrochallenge-2024-10/>) taking the grand prize. We already knew that [Robert Price](<https://www.robertprice.co.uk/robblog/category/rc2024/>) won the RC2014 category, which got him an original set of RC2014 PCBs and a Classic II, but it is great to see so many familiar names win prizes or get an honourable mention. Congratulations to everybody that took part, particularly those that made it to the finish line.
