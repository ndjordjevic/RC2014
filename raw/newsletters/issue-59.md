## 16C550 Serial Module

With the Zilog SIO/2 serial chip reaching End Of Life last year a replacement is needed before stock of those chips run out.  The 16C550 UART is an excellent chip and already has full support in RomWBW.  
The [16C550 Single UART Module](<https://z80kits.com/shop/16c550-single-uart-serial-module/>) was released at the end of 2024 and can work on any of the 8 address ports that RomWBW supports, and can even work alongside 68B50 or SIO/2 modules to add more serial I/O to your RomWBW machine.   
|  |  ![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/69c263de-db09-1dbf-c8a2-5286c283dc33.jpg)  
---  
The [16C2550 Dual UART Module](<https://z80kits.com/shop/16c2550-dual-uart-serial-module/>) launched earlier in January does everything its little brother does, but with the benefit of having two serial ports, both of which can have their address ports set separately.

## Sharp MZ-80K

Is there a Sharp MZ-80K hiding inside your RC2014? Well, if you have an RP2040 VGA Terminal there could be! [Tim Holyoake](<https://z80.timholyoake.uk/pico-mz-80k-and-the-rc2014-rp2040-vga-terminal-card/>) has built an emulator for this classic Z80 machine that runs on an RP2040. Just download the .uf2 file from [GitHub](<https://github.com/psychotimmy/picomz-80k/releases/tag/1.2.3>) and drop it on to the card via USB.  
The only question we have is when will the MZ-80A be ready? :-)

## DTFM Codes on an RC2014

Not only did Rob Price build a DTFM (telephone keypad tone) [decoder module](<https://www.robertprice.co.uk/robblog/decoding-dtmf-tones-on-an-rc2014/>), but he also used his AY-3-8910 module to [make DTFM tones](<https://www.robertprice.co.uk/robblog/generating-dtmf-tones-using-an-ay-3-8910-on-a-rc2014/>). Along with the keypad, I suspect smart phones can finally become smart with a Z80 powering them!
