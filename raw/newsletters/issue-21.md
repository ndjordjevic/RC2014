## VCF West 2019

A Vintage Computer Federation event is the annual highlight for anybody that enjoys the old hardware, and [VCF West](http://vcfed.org/wp/festivals/vintage-computer-festival-west/) has been on my radar for quite a few years now.  Sadly, I'm still not able to make it there this year either - however, that doesn't mean that it'll be devoid of RC2014 goodness!  Longstanding RC2014 supporters Jay Cotton and Tom  Szolyga will have a booth there with a whole range of different RC2014 setups.  So if you're lucky enough to be near Mountain View, California on 3rd or 4th August, make sure you pay them a visit.

## OSHCamp 2019

Just a quick reminder that [OSHCamp](http://oshug.org/event/oshcamp2019) is being held at the end of August in Hebden Bridge, and is a fantastic weekend for those of you in to Open Source Hardware.  
  
RC2014 is sponsoring the event, and attendees there will be the first to get their hands on the new RC2014 Micro, so snap up a ticket quick!

## Liverpool MakeFest 2019

It was another fun time at [Liverpool MakeFest](https://lpoolmakefest.org/) this year, which has now become the UKs biggest maker event.  It was great to meet so many of you face to face, even if a few of you did have to stop me mid-spiel to say "Yes, I know what the RC2014 is - I have one already!" :-)

## London Retro Meetup Group

After searching for, and failing to find a Meetup group in London* that covered the RC2014, Dave Snowdon decided to set up his own. [Sign up now](https://www.meetup.com/London-Retro-Computing/) to be notified of upcoming meetings.  
* That's London, UK, not London, Ontario ;-)
