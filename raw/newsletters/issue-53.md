## Z80 End Of Life / Last Chance To Buy

This newsletter starts with the sad news that Zilog has decided to cease production of the Z80 CPU, along with the Z180 and all the associated peripheral chips like the SIO/2. The last chance to place orders will be June 14th with the final chips rolling off the production line some months after that.  
This is quite a blow for the retro community and potentially devastating news for z80kits.com and anybody else that makes their living from supplies of this little CPU. Luckily  the outlook for RC2014 in the short-medium term is pretty good. Longer term will depend on how many chips are made in the final run and the availibility of them after that.  
You can read more on my take of the situation on [rc2014.co.uk](<https://rc2014.co.uk/2757/zilog-z80-end-of-life-notification/>)

## Mini II Proto Board

  
Continuing with the new product every month, April saw the launch of the[ Mini II Proto Board](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-mini-ii-proto-board/>). This handy prototyping board fits the original Mini or [Mini II](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-mini-ii/>), with or without the CP/M Upgrade Kit. In addition to the 926 pads of protyping area, there are breakouts for LED, switch, serial and also expansion slots.

## Easter Egg Hunt Roundup

Thank you to those of you that played along with the Easter Egg Hunt from the last newsletter. For those that want to check how they did, then clues gave you **5A38304B4954533135**. Splitting these in to pairs of hex digits gives you the ASCII for **Z80KITS15** which was the code needed. Well done to everybody that got it right!

## Coming Soon - LCD Module

Later this month the new [LCD Driver Module](<https://z80kits.com/shop/lcd-driver-module/>) will be released.  This can support 2x8 and 2x16 character displays on the module itself, or remotely run up to a 4x20 character - Ideal if your RC2014 is mounted in an enclosure. The final PCBs are due in a few days and the module will be for sale later this week.
