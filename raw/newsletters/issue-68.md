## Coming Soon - RC2014 Zed Mini

This little powerhouse puts all the power of the RC2014 Zed in to form factor of the RC2014 Mini. Armed with 512k RAM and 512k ROM, and a 16C550 UART, this Z80 based single board computer makes an ideal base for a compact RomWBW machine.   
|  |  [ ![](https://mcusercontent.com/9794c5b78f76285b2bc1ff7bd/images/55abb21c-2564-6ca5-bb15-a697b1e9f1de.jpg) ](<https://www.tindie.com/products/shieladixon/tmsemu3-graphics-card-designed-for-rc2014/>)  
---  
The single Enhanced Bus expansion slot lets you add the storage medium of your choice, or add a Backplane 5 for 5 slots of possibility.  
  
The RC2014 Zed Mini is due for launch by the end of June on [z80kits](<https://z80kits.com/>)

## ZBot80 Coming Soon

The most common complaint about the RC2014 kits is that you can't drive them around the kitchen or chase the dog with them. Well, that is about to change with the launch of the [ZBot80](<https://4tronix.co.uk/blog/?p=2324>) next month. This buggy chassis was designed by [4Tronix](<https://shop.4tronix.co.uk/collections/robot-kits>), one of the finest builders of robot kits.  The 4 motors are powered by a pair of 18650 batteries, and the brains come from an [RC2014 Mini II](<https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-mini-ii/>) (Or RC2014 Zed Mini!). It is easily programmed from BASIC, Z80 Assembly, FORTH, or anything that can send an OUT command.

## RC2014 Assembly 2.0

Last year was the inaugural [RC2014 Assembly 1.0](<https://rc2014.co.uk/3855/rc2014-assembly-v1-0/>) at The National Museum of Computing. This was an absolute success and a lot of people have asked if it will be happening again. The answer is yes! [RC2014 Assembly 2.0](<https://www.eventbrite.co.uk/e/rc2014-assembly-20-tickets-1992559683652>) September 26th 2026 is the date for your diary. TNMOC is a fanstastic venue, and the staff and volunteers there are all amazing so I'm sure it will be another amazing day.
