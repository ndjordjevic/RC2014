## Ukrainian Fundraiser

I am sure we have all seen the awful situation over in Ukraine and want to do what we can to help. Unfortunately I have very few ways that I can help. However, I can make awesome retro computer kits.  And awesome retro computer kits can make money. And money can help charities save lives.  
  
So I have a short run of 20 [RC2014 Classic II](https://z80kits.com/shop/ukraine-red-cross-fundraiser-rc2014-classic-ii/) kits and 20 [RC2014 Zed](https://z80kits.com/shop/ukraine-red-cross-fundraiser-rc2014-zed/) kits that are themed in the colours of the Ukrainian flag. They are no different from the standard models, and are priced exactly the same. However, 50% of the sale price of these two yellow and blue kits will be donated to a humanitarian charity.  
  
Since their launch earlier this week, 35 kits have been sold, raising £1627.50   
|  |  ![](RC2014%20Newsletter%20-%20Issue%2041_files/d1575fc0-f4ae-3582-d5ed-9d281b45e06d.jpg)  
---  
Currently the Classic II is sold out, but once all of the initial 40 kits are shipped (Just waiting for some extra components on Monday to finish shipping the last few) I hope to release another smaller batch.

## Pi Pico VGA Terminal Firmware Update

At launch, the[ Pi Pico VGA Terminal](https://z80kits.com/shop/rc2014-pi-pico-vga-terminal/) worked great, but keyboard support was a bit hit and miss. Thanks to a firmware update, all keyboards we've tested are now working fine.  
  
If your Pi Pico VGA Terminal isn't currently working with your favourite keyboard, grab the latest 1.0 .uf2  from [GitHub](https://github.com/RC2014Z80/picoterm) and everything should work fine.

## Tiny RC2040 32x24mm!

How do you make an RC2014 the size of a postage stamp? Simple, emulate it on a Pi Pico! That's just what Extreme Electronics did. There's a writeup [here](https://www.extremeelectronics.co.uk/emulating-a-z80-rc2014-with-cpm-and-ide-drives-via-an-sd-card/) and the code is up on [GitHub](https://github.com/ExtremeElectronics/RC2040)
