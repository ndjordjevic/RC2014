# New Additions

The RC2014 range has now been expanded with the [RC2014 Plus](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/rc2014-plus-homebrew-z80-computer-kit/) and the [RC2014 Pro](https://www.tindie.com/products/Semachthemonkey/rc2014-pro-homebrew-z80-computer-kit/) models.  The Plus features 32k RAM and SIO/2 serial on an 8 slot backplane, whilst the Pro has 64k, Pageable ROM and runs CP/M from compact flash

# Hackaday Unconference, Retro Computer Festival and more!

September was a busy month with the [Hackaday Bring a Hack](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/hackaday-bring-a-hack-meetup-uk-tickets-37313388349), the [Hackaday Unconference](https://hackaday.com/2017/08/06/hackaday-uk-unconference-needs-you/) and the [Retro Computer Festival](http://www.computinghistory.org.uk/det/43210/Retro-Computer-Festival-2017-16th-17th-September-2017/) all happening at the same weekend.  I think I counted at least 15 RC2014 owners  that I got to meet that weekend, which was great!  
The Centre for Computing History made a short video about the RC2014 whilst I was at the Retro Computer Festival, so I've included it here for your viewing pleasure :-)

# Mass Storage for RC2014

Designer Ed Brindley, the brains behind the [AY Soundcard](https://www.tindie.com/products/edbrindley/ymay-sound-card-pcb-for-the-rc2014-computer/) for RC2014 has done it again with this [IDE Adapter](https://www.tindie.com/products/edbrindley/ide-adapter-pcb-for-the-rc2014-computer/) that's designed for RC2014.  If you're in need of extra storage options, it's certainly worth checking out!
