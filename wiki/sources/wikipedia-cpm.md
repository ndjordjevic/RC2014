## What it covers
- CP/M (Control Program for Microcomputers): created 1974 by Gary Kildall at Digital Research; the dominant microcomputer OS of the late 1970s–mid 1980s.
- Architecture: BIOS (hardware), BDOS (file system/services), CCP (command interpreter); programs are transient `.COM` files.
- Versions: CP/M 2.2 (most deployed), CP/M 3.0/Plus (1983, bank-switched memory). Ran on 8080/8085 and Z80; later CP/M-86 / CP/M-68K.
- Significance: portable software library; displaced by MS-DOS; still active in retrocomputing (RC2014), now open-source.
