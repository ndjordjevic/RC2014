**Index entry.** rc2014.co.uk is the official home and shop of the RC2014, a simple 8-bit Z80 modular computer (8K ROM, 32K RAM, 7.3728 MHz, serial at 115,200 baud) originally built to run Microsoft BASIC. This page indexes what the site offers; deeper dives into individual kits, modules, or guides can be added later.

_All claims below are sourced from ../../raw/web/rc2014.co.uk.md unless otherwise noted._

## What the site offers
A catalog of RC2014 kits and bus modules, downloadable assembly guides, troubleshooting help, and links to the official GitHub org for code. Top nav: Assembly Guides · Full Kits · Backplanes and Enclosures · Modules · Contact · Troubleshooting · File Packager · File Unpackager.

## Full kits
RC2014 Classic II · RC2014 Micro · RC2014 Mini · RC2014 Mini CP/M Upgrade · RC2014 Mini II · RC2014 Mini II CP/M Upgrade Kit · RC2014 Mini II Picasso · RC2014 Orton 3C · RC2014 Pro Pride and Zed Pro Pride · Universal Micro Keyboard. (The RC2014 Micro is a 32K Z80 single-board variant running MS-BASIC or the Small Computer Monitor; see its z80kits listing and assembly-guide PDF, indexed separately.)

## Modules (selected)
16C550 Twin UART · 512k ROM/RAM · CH375 USB Storage · Compact Flash · Digital I/O · Dual Clock & Reset · Serial modules · ESP8266 WiFi · IDE Hard Drive · LCD Driver · Pageable ROM · Pi Pico / RP2040 VGA Terminal · SID-Ulator Sound · Z80 CPU v2.1 · 64k RAM, plus Retired/Other modules.

## Assembly guides & documentation (PDFs)
Per-kit guides (Classic II, Mini II, Mini II Picasso/CP/M upgrade, Micro, Pro tips, Zed/Zed Pro), jumper-settings references, and older kit guides — all hosted as downloadable PDFs linked from /assembly-guides/.

## Software & firmware options
The site documents four firmware environments for the machine: **Microsoft BASIC 4.7** (Grant Searle's NASCOM-derived BASIC, 32k/56k), **CP/M 2.2** (Grant Searle, loads from Compact Flash), **RomWBW** (CP/M-80/Z-System for 512k ROM/RAM machines, disk/video/VT-100), and the **Small Computer Monitor** (Steve Cousins' Z80 monitor with assembler/disassembler). See the site's comparison article for which to choose.
